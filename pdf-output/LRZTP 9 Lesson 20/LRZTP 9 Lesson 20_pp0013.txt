b) "Too …", using དྲགས་ (also: དྲག་)
The syllable དྲགས་ is added to the root of an adjective to make it into an excessive, meaning
"too …". Here, དྲགས་ should be thought of as being a verb and should be used and conjugated
accordingly. For example:
ངས་ཁ་ལག་མང་པོ་ཞེ་དྲགས་བཟས་པ་ཡིན།
I ate too much food.
ཆང་མང་པོ་ཞེ་དྲགས་མ་འཐུང་ཨ།
Do not drink too much chang!
གལ་སིད་མི་མང་པོ་སེབས་ན་བསྡད་ས་ཉུང་དྲགས་ཀི་རེད།
If a lot of people come, there won't be enough seats (lit. the seats will be too few).
ཅ་ལག་འདི་གོང་ཆེ་དྲགས་(ཀི་)འདུག This thing is too expensive.
c) Asking questions, using ལོད་
The syllable ལོད་ is used in order to ask how much or to quantify the adjective. It is added to the
root of the adjective. Just as with the question words in Lesson 5, no question particle (པས་, གས་,
ངས་) is required – although some Tibetans use one anyway. Be aware that ལོད་ is only used for
questions (both direct and indirect). If you wish to make an exclamation, such as “How tasty these
momos are!” – then you need to use གང་འདྲས་). For example:
མདང་དགོང་གསུང་ཆོས་ལ་མི་མང་ལོད་འདུག
མི་མང་ཐག་ཆོད་འདུག
How many people were at the teaching yesterday? Very many!
འཛིན་གྲྭ་ལ་སིད་ལོད་བྱུང་།
སིད་ཐག་ཆོད་བྱུང་།
How much fun was it at school? It was loads of fun!
