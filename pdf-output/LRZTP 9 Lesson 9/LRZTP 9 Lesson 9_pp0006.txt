གོང་གཅོག་པ།1
གོང་ཆག་པ།

to sell (ཐ་དད་པ།)
as in: ཚོང་དཔོན་ = seller
to do business (ཐ་དད་པ།)
ཚོང་ = to sell, business; རྒྱག་པ་= to do, verbalizer used with many
nouns
to buy, to purchase (ཐ་དད་པ།)
to buy, to purchase, to look at (H) (ཐ་དད་པ།)
གཟིགས་ = to see (H), as in: སན་རས་གཟིགས་ = Avalokiteshvara
to be too expensive (ཐ་མི་དད་པ།)
གོང་ = price; ཆེ་ = big; དགས་པ་ = excessive syllable, as in:
མང་དགས་པ་ = to be too much
to return/to bring back something (ཐ་དད་པ།)
ཚུར་ = back; སོག་པ་ = to return something, as in: ཨ་སོག་རྒྱག་པ་ =
to turn upside down
to increase the price (ཐ་དད་པ།)
གོང་ = price; སར་བ་ = to increase
(Note for grammar enthusiasts: The ས་མགོ་ indicates that the
verb is ཐ་དད་པ་ - see Lesson 29)
the price going up, to increase price (ཐ་མི་དད་པ།)
གོང་ = price; འཕར་བ་= to go up, to rise
(Note for grammar enthusiasts: The neutral letter ཕ་ indicates
that the verb is ཐ་མི་དད་པ་ – see Lesson 29)
to give a discount, lower the price, to bargain (ཐ་དད་པ།)
གོང་ = price; བཅག་པ་ = to break
the price going down, decrease price (ཐ་མི་དད་པ།)
གོང་ = price; ཆག་པ་ = to be broken, to break

1

This is another case, where people, when they talk, like to use the past tense for the present, so they
say: བཅག་པ་ rather than: གཅོག་པ་
