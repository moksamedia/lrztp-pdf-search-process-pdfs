The usage here is particularly in the context of “we” and the person answering is part
of this group of people and therefore will answer using the auxiliaries pertaining to བདག.
For example:
སང་ཉིན་ང་ཚོ་ཚང་མ་ཟ་ཁང་ལ་འགོ་གས།
Shall we all go to the restaurant tomorrow?
This question has a sense of suggestion - and the answer might be:
ཏག་ཏག་རེད། འགོ།(འགོ་དོ)།
Perfect! Let’s go!
སང་ཉིན་ང་ཚོ་ཁ་ལག་ག་རེ་བཟོ་ག
What food are we going to prepare tomorrow?
Also in this sentence, the ག expresses that the person addressed is most likely part of the
“we”. The answer might be:
གང་ཤེས། ང་ཚོ་མོག་མོག་བཟོས་ན་གང་འདྲས་འདུག
I do not know. What about if we make momos?
And the conversation might go on with...
ཤ་ཡོད་མོག་མོག་བཟོ་ག ཤ་མེད་མོག་མོག་བཟོ་ག
Shall we make non-veg momos or veg momos?
In the above questions, this abbreviated form is commonly used; however, it could be
replaced by གི་ཡིན། or གི་ཡིན་པས། if the person answering is part of the group.
ང་ཚོ་སང་ཉིན་ཟ་ཁང་ལ་འགོ་གི་ཡིན་པས།
Are we going to the restaurant tomorrow?
Shall I? Should I? What if I…?
One last case where we use this abbreviated form for just oneself is when it has the sense
of a suggestion. For example:
ངས་ཁེད་རང་ལ་རོགས་པ་བྱེད་ག
Shall I help you?
ངས་རོ་ལགས་ཡར་བསང་ག
Should I lift up the granny?
ངས་པུ་གུ་ལ་རན་པ་ག་རེ་ཉོ་ག
