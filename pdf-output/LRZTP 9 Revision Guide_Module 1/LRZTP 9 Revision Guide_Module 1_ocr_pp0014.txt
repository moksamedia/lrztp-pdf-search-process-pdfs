Imperative

To make an imperative you can simply just use the present tense form of a verb without any auxiliary. You
can use WW’ with people you’re close with or to emphasize something important, or དང་ which functions a bit
like a “!” and really encourages the person addressed to do X.
ང་ལ་སྒྱུ་གུ་འདི་སྤྲད་དང་།

Give me this pen, please!

The verbs aay and dic: have special forms, namely རྒྱུག་ = go (away!) and ཤོག = come!
The boundaries between honorific etc. are pretty fluent, so that you will sometimes hear:
གསུང་དང་།

Please tell (me)! (H.)

ང་ལ་རོགས་པ་གནང་ཨ།

Please help me! (H.)

ངའི་སྐྱེ་སྐར་ཐུགས་སྤྲོ་ལ་ཕེབས་ཤོག

Please come to my birthday party!

Let’s do X!
If you want to say “let’s do X” - you’ll add a དོ་ to your verb and sometimes start your sentence with
ད་ = now, 80, then...

ད་ཁ་ལག་ཟ་དོ།

So, let’s eat!

ད་འཛིན་གྲྭ་ལ་འགྲོ་དོ།

Then, let’s go to class!

ད་མཉམ་དུ་འགྲོ་དོ།

So, let’s go together!

With non-volitional verbs, we use a special construction to say: “Don’t forget X!”, for example: “don’t lose

Word (+ a") + Verb + པར་བྱེད་ (+1)
