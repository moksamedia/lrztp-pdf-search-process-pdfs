5) Present perfect
We use it when we talk about the result of a past action.
This form consists of nothing else than the past form of the verb + "to have."

ངས་མོག་མོག་བཟོས་ཡོད། I've made momos (and here they are).
ཟ་ཁང་འདེ་ལ་གོང་འཕར་འདུག / བཞག

IRAN

The prices this restaurant have gone up.

SAR] AR BAR GAARA བཞག

ཨོ། གངས་མང་པོ་བབས་འདུ་ག/ བཞག

(S)he has bought a variety of stuff.

Oh, there has been lots of snowfall.

Remember that བཞག is only for positive statements, not for negations and questions...
ཨོ། འོ་མ་རྫོགས་བཞག = Oh, we're out of milk!

གངས་བབས་མི་འདུག

Is there snow on the mountain? No. (Lit. has

snow fallen on the mountain)
So, to summarize the two past tenses:
གངས་མང་པོ་བབས་སོང་། (direct experience of the action)

It snowed a lot.

གངས་མང་པོ་བབས་པ་རེད། (inferential knowledge of the action) It snowed a lot.
གངས་མང་པོ་བབས་འདུག / བཞག (direct experience of the result) There has been lots of snowfall (over
night for example).
གངས་མང་པོ་བབས་ཡོད་རེད། (inferential knowledge of the result) There has been lots of snowfall (over
night for example).
6) The Particle གར་
When we say in English "to go to do X" or "to come for doing Y" - we use the syllable གར་ in Tibetan.
So, the formula is:

Verb (present tense) + གར་ + Verb going/coming in any time or mode

ཆང་ཁང་ལ་གཞས་གཏོང་

གར་

Sing songs at the bar to

ཡོང་གི་ཡིན་པས།
are you coming?

Are you coming to sing songs at the bar?
