therefore does not have any (notable) effect on the pronunciation of the word. There are two

THE THREE HEAD (SUPERSCRIBED) LETTERS, མགློ་ཅན་གསུམ།
Three of the thirty consonants, གསལ་བེད་ can appear at the head, མགློ་ཅན་ of the main letter,
མིང་གཞི་. The word མགློ་ means head. These three letters are ར་, ལ་ and ས་. When superscribed
in this way, they are called: ར་མགློ་, ལ་མགློ་ and ས་མགློ་.
As for the sound, head letters, མགློ་ཅན་ are silent like the prefixes, སྔློན་འཇུག་ and have the
same effect on the letters (of the first five rows) of the 3rd and 4th column. They thus remove
the aspiration from column 3 and convert the letters of column 4 to high tone. For example:
ང་ (low tone):
I, me
ལྔ་ (high tone):
five
ན་ (low tone):
to be sick རྣ་ (high tone):
ear
གློ་ (slightly aspirated): to hear
སློ་ (non aspirated):
door
