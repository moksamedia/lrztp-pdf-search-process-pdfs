THE FIVE PREFIXES སྔློན་འཇུག་ལྔ།
Five of the thirty consonants, གསལ་བེད་ can take the role of a prefix, སྔློན་འཇུག. These are all
female letters.
A prefix, སྔློན་འཇུག་ precedes the main letter, as indicated by its name – in both Tibetan and
English. Prefixes, སྔློན་འཇུག་ are (mostly) mute, which means that it is important to memorize
which one applies.
The five prefixes, སྔློན་འཇུག་ are as follows:
ག་
ད་
བ་
མ་
འ
It may help you to memorize these prefixes by creating a little mnemonic, such as “George
Drives By Mary Always.”
The sillier your sentence, the better it will stick in your mind! An interesting point for
grammar enthusiasts and those who like rules: there are indigenous Tibetan grammar texts
which provide lists of the prefixes which precede particular main letters, མིང་གཞི་ For precise
pronunciation, it is important to know that all prefixes, སྔློན་འཇུག་ have an influence on the
sound of certain main letters, མིང་གཞི། For example:
གློ་ (slightly aspirated): to hear
མགློ་ (not aspirated): head
ཇ་ (slightly aspirated): tea
འཇུག་ (not aspirated): to enter
ད་ (slightly aspirated ): now
བདག་ (not aspirated): self
Looking at the alphabet table, you can see that the above three letters (ག་, ཇ་, ད་) are all from
the 3rd column (female letters).
