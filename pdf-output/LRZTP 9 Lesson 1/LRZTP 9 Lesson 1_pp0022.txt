HOW TO IDENTIFY THE MAIN LETTER, མིང་གཞི་OF A SYLLABLE
The first step in being able to read is to identify the main letter མིང་གཞི་ of a syllable. There
are a few rules and clues, as follows:
a)
Whichever letter has a vowel དབངས་, head letter མགློ་ཅན་ or attached letter འདློགས་ཅན་
MUST be the main letter མིང་གཞི་. For example:
མེ་ཏློག་ : flower
མ་ and ཏ་ each have a vowel དབངས་ and are both main letters, མིང་གཞི་.
b)
Whenever there are only two letters and no vowel, དབངས་, then the first is the main
letter, མིང་གཞི་ and the second is the suffix, རེས་འཇུག་. For example:
དང་ : and ད་ is the main letter, མིང་གཞི་ , ང་ is the suffix, རེས་འཇུག་ .
c)
Whenever there are three letters, the middle one is normally the main letter, མིང་གཞི་.
For example:
བཀའ་: speech
བ་ is the prefix, སྔློན་འཇུག་,
ཀ་ is the main letter, མིང་གཞི་,
འ་ is the suffix, རེས་འཇུག་.
d)
When the final letter is the ཡང་འཇུག་ (second suffix), ས་, then the first letter is the
main letter, མིང་གཞི , the second the suffix, རེས་འཇུག་ and the third the second suffix,
ཡང་འཇུག. For example:
གངས་: snow
ག་ is the main letter མིང་གཞི་, ང་ is the suffix རེས་འཇུག་, ས is the
second suffix ཡང་འཇུག་.
e)
When there are four letters in a syllable written one after the other, the second is the
main letter, མིང་གཞི་. This is because there is no third suffix. For example:
བསགས་པ་: accumulated
བ་ is the prefix, སྔློན་འཇུག་,
ས་ is the main letter, མིང་གཞི་,
ག་ is the suffix, རེས་འཇུག་,
ས is the second suffix, ཡང་འཇུག་
