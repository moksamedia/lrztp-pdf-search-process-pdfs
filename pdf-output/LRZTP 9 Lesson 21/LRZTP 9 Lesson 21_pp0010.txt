Temporal connectives
This lesson introduces the temporal connectives of simultaneity: དུས་, སབས་ལ་, ཞློར་ལ་, རིང་ལ་, ཤུལ་
རིང་ཀ་ and གིན་་་་གིན།.
a) དུས།, ཤུལ་རིང་ཀ། and གིན་་་་གིན།
The above connectives always follow after a Present Tense verb. Their translation into English may
be quite flexible, but the different meanings of the Tibetan words should be preserved.
དུས།
The connective དུས་ means "time", so it can be translated as "when", "at the time", etc. The
sentence can be expressed in the past, present or future tenses and the second verb can also be
expressed in any tense, with any person or in any of the modes. For example:
དེ་རིང་ཞློགས་གས་ཆར་པ་འབབ་དུས་ང་ནང་ལ་བསྡད་པ་ཡིན།
This morning when it was raining, I stayed at home.
ཁ་སང་ང་ཚོ་འཛིན་གྲྭ་ལ་ཡློད་དུས་གངས་མང་པློ་བབས་སློང་།
While we were in class yesterday, there was a heavy snowfall.
ཁ་སིང་ངའི་ཨ་མས་ཁློམ་ལ་ཉློ་ཆ་རྒྱག་དུས་ཁློང་ལ་གློགས་མློ་རིང་པ་ཅིག་ཐུག་སློང་།
When my mother was shopping the other day at the market, she met an old friend.
བློ་བཟང་གཟུགས་པློ་འཁྲུད་དུས་གློག་ཕིན་སློང་།
While Lobsang was having a shower, the electricity went off.
According to some Tibetans...
Although sentences with དུས་ often relate to the past, no བེད་སྒྲ་ (agentive particle) is required to
mark the agent of the verb that precedes the དུས་. For example:
ཁེ་ས་ང་གཞས་བཏང་བསྡད་དུས་ངའི་ཨ་མ་ལགས་ནང་ལ་ཕེབས་སློང་།
Yesterday, while I was singing a song, my dear mother came home.
However, if I just said “Yesterday I sang a song,” then a བེད་སྒྲ་ would be required. For
example:
ཁེ་ས་ངས་གཞས་བཏང་པ་ཡིན།
Yesterday I sang a song.
The reason for this usage is that in the first example the main topic is the time of singing the
song, rather than the action itself.
