བྱེད་སྒྲ་ The Agentive Particle
The ལ་དོན་༢༠6 the འབྲེལ་སྒྲ་ particles are not too foreign to our ears because they have an equivalent in English.
Conversely, the agentive particle བྱེད་སྒྲ་, does not have an English equivalent and is therefore a bit harder to get used to.
The agentive particle བྱེད་སྒྲ་ is used in Tibetan in order to mark the doer and/or the instrument of an action.

Usage Rules:
Whilst in literary Tibetan, the བྱེད་སྒྲ་ is used in all three times (past, present and future), the rule for colloquial usage is the

following:
1)

In the past tense, the བྱེད་སྒྲ་ should/must be used with བྱ་བྱེད་ཐ་དད་པ་ verbs.

2)

When it is used in the present/future tense or with བྱ་བྱེད་ཐ་མི་དད་པ་ verbs in the past, the བྱེད་སྒྲ་ emphasizes the doer.
