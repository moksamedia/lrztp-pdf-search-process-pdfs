The Conditional: “If…”
The use of the conditional is very common in Tibetan and therefore of the utmost
importance. The hardest part is probably getting used to the fact that the "if" is positioned in
the middle of the Tibetan sentence. Furthermore, it is important to remember that only
auxiliaries related to བདག (i.e. ཡིན་, མིན་, ཡོད་ and མེད་) are used in conditional constructions.
The formula:
Condition + ན་+ rest of the sentence
This usage of the conditional makes a distinction between something that is possible and
something that is unreal. For example:

Possible:
Unreal:

"If you have time, please help me!"
"If she had had the time, she would have helped me."

To add extra emphasis to the conditional aspect, one begins the sentence with གལ་སིད་.
a) The Conditional, with the verbs "to have" and "to be"
གལ་སིད་ཁེད་རང་དབྱིན་ཇི་ཡིན་ན་དབྱིན་སྐད་ཤེས་ཀི་ཡོད་རེད།
If you are a Westerner, you (already) know English.
གལ་སིད་ཁོང་ཧིན་དུ་མིན་ན་ལྷ་ཁང་འདི་ལ་འགོ་ཆོག་གི་མ་རེད།
If she is not a Hindu, she is not allowed to enter this temple.
Note: ཆོག means "to be allowed"; it is a secondary verb. See Lesson 25.
ང་ལ་དུས་ཚོད་རག་ན་ཐུགས་སོ་ལ་ཡོང་གི་ཡིན།
If I have time, I will come to the party.
ཁེད་རང་ལ་དངུལ་མེད་ན་ངས་གཡར་ཆོག
If you don't have any money, I'll lend you some.
