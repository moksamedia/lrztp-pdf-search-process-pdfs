b) The Conditional and the Present tense
For the structure to be grammatically correct, any verb that precedes ན་ should be in the Past
tense. This is because the condition always precedes the consequence (1.e. the second part of
the sentence). However, in spoken Tibetan the Present tense 18 sometimes used. For example:
ཟ་ཁང་འདིར་ཁ་ལག་ཟ་ན་གྲོད་ཁོག་ན་གི་རེད།
If you eat in this restaurant, you’ll get a stomach ache.
It is much better to say:

ཟ་ཁང་འདད་ཁ་ལག་བཟས་ནྲ་་ད་ཁག་ནབ་རད།
