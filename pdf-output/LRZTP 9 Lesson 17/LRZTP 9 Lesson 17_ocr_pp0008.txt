Adjectives
a) General remarks
In the Tibetan language, a word is not inherently a noun, verb or adjective. It can only be classified
as a part of speech depending on its usage within the sentence.
བློ་བཟང་མི་ཡག་པོ་ཅིག་རེད།

Lobsang is a nice guy.

ཆང་མ་བཏུང་ན་ཡག་ས་རེད།

It seems better not to drink chang.

The ཡག་ is the same in both sentences. However, in the first sentence, with the syllable 2 added, it

functions as an adjective —meaning “good.” In the second sentence, the same syllable ཡག, this time
functioning as a verb meaning “‘to be better,”1s conjugated with an auxiliary of probability. These
examples illustrate that it is only a particular usage within the sentence that turns a syllable into an
adjective or verb.
Another peculiarity of adjectives in Tibetan is the way they are added to what we would define
as nouns, thereby forming new adjectives.
