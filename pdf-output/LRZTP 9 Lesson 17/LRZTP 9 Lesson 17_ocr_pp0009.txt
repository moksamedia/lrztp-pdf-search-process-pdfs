b) The Comparative
As in English, adjectives in Tibetan can have three different modes — positive, comparative and
superlative. Examples of these would be “new”,

“newer” and “newest”, respectively. Note: The

“positive” is just the simple adjective, as found in the dictionary.

How to form the comparative
In order to form the comparative in Tibetan, one takes the root of the adjective. This is simply the
adjective without its final syllable. For example:
