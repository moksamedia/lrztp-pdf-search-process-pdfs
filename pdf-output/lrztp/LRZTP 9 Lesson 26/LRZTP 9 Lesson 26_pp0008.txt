Grammatical aims of this lesson
After studying this lesson you will know:
• the verb ཚར་བ། : "to be finished" and “to have already done X”
• the expression: “to make sure that X will happen”
• how to say “almost X”, "about to do X"
• how to say "just done X"

a)

ཚར་བ། – to finish, to have already done X

The verb ཚར་བ། can function as either a full or a secondary verb.
ཚར་བ། as a full verb
As a full verb, ཚར་བ། means "to finish" or "to end." It is perceived as a non-volitional verb and
conjugated accordingly. For example:
ཁེད་རང་རང་སོང་མགོགས་པོ་བས་ན་ལམ་སང་ཚར་གི་རེད།
If you do your homework quickly, you'll finish (it) in an instant.
ལྟད་མོ་འདི་ཆུ་ཚོད་གཉིས་ཀི་ནང་ལ་ཚར་གི་མེད་འགོའོ།
This performance will probably finish within two hours.
འཛིན་གྲྭ་ཚར་སོང་། ད་ནང་ལ་རེད་མོ་རེ་གར་འགོ་(དོ)།
Class is over – so let's go home and play games!
ཚར་བ། as a secondary verb
As a secondary verb, ཚར་བ། is added to a Past tense verb. It is most commonly translated as
"already" or "not yet." Its conjugation is exclusively in the Past tense, and the པ་ is generally
dropped. For example:
ཁེད་རང་གིས་དེ་རིང་གི་ཚགས་པར་བལྟས་ཚར་ཡིན་པས།
