Here, the first བེད་ས་, namely གིས་, marks the main agent of the action - Lobsang.
The second བེད་ས་, namely ས་ added to the word ལག་པ་, indicates the subsidiary agent, the instrument of the action his hands.
As illustrated by earlier examples, most sentences only have a main agent བེད་པ་པོ་གཙོ་བོ།.
As shown by the most recent example, however, some sentences have both agent and instrument, བེད་པ་པོ་གཙོ་བོ་ and

Although there is only one བེད་ས་ in this sentence, this does not necessarily mean that it is the བེད་པ་པོ་གཙོ་བོ་, the
main agent. By definition, the བེད་པ་པོ་གཙོ་བོ་ needs to be endowed with volition. This is clearly not the case here, as a
pen does not have volition and must therefore be the instrument – the བེད་པ་པོ་ཕལ་བ།.
