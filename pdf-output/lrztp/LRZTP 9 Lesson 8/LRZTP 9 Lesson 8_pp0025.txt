A Philosophical Side-Note:
In terms of the concept of བདག་གཞན་, the future tense is inherently linked with གཞན་
whereas the present tense is related to བདག་. Maybe this is why the auxiliaries related to
གཞན་ are used to express the inherent nature of something? It reflects that this individual
does not have any choice; it functions in a certain way due to its nature. It reflects the idea
that we take care of what is closer to us, i.e. the present. This notion is expressed in the
Tibetan word བདག་པོ་རྒྱག་པ་, which means “to take care of / look after / to preserve / to
posses.” The future, on the other hand, since it is further away, is more in the hands of
གཞན་ (i.e. other conditions.)
Other Future Auxiliaries
There are two more common future auxiliaries – the common ཡག་ and rarely used མཁན་.
These replace the particle གི་, ཀི་, གི་. For example, instead of saying:
དོ་དགོང་ང་ནང་ལ་ལོག་གི་ཡིན། Tonight I will return home.
You would say:
དོ་དགོང་ང་ནང་ལ་ལོག་ཡག་ཡིན། or: དོ་དགོང་ང་ནང་ལ་ལོག་མཁན་ཡིན།
Tonight, I will return home.
Even though they carry the slightly different notions explained below, in terms of English
translation they are identical.
ལོ་རེས་མར་ཁོང་བོད་ལ་ཕེབས་ཡག་མ་རེད་པས།
Isn't he going to Tibet next year? (H)
ལགས་རེད། ལོ་རེས་མར་ཁོང་ཁོ་རང་གི་ནང་མི་དང་མཉམ་དུ་བོད་ལ་གནས་སྐོར་ལ་ཕེབས་ཡག་རེད།
Yes, he is. Next year, he will go to Tibet for a pilgrimage with his family. (H)
སང་ཉིན་ང་རྒྱལ་ས་ལ་འགོ་མཁན་ཡིན། དེ་རིང་ང་ཐུགས་སྤྲོ་ལ་འགོ་མཁན་མིན།
Tomorrow, I'll go to town. Today, I won't go to the party.
