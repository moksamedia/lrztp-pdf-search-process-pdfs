Volunteering
There is a particular way of indicating that the speaker will perform the action of the verb on
behalf of, in favour of, instead of, or for, someone else. Since these actions carry a sense of

future, they are presented here. There are three auxiliaries that are used to express this: དགོས་›

ཆོག ཐ16ཡོང་།, (Note: the umlaut in དགོས་ is usually not pronounced; it sounds more like: དགོ་)
Their usage is limited to the first person. Because one is insisting on doing something, the
first person “I” is emphasized by the use of the བྱེད་སྒྲ་, the agentive marker. For example:

ལས་ཀ་འདི་ངས་བྱེད་དགོས།

Twill do this work (instead of you.)

ངས་ཏོ་ཆ་བྱརྒྱབ་ཆོག

Iwill do the shopping (for you.)

ཚོགས་འདུ་ལ་ངས་ཕྱིན་ཆོག

Iwill go to the meeting (on your behalf.)

འོ་མ་ངས་ཁྱེར་ཡོང་དགོས།

Twill come and bring milk.
