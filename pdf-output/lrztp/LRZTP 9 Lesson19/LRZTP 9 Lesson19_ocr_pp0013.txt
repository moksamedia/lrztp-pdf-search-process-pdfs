The above sentence is very similar to the example in Lesson 18:

ང་ལ་དུག་སློག་འཁྲུ་ཡག་མང་པོ་ཡོད།
I have lots of clothes which need to be washed.
The meaning is almost the same, but the sentence structure is slightly different. The difference lies in
the emphasis. In Tibetan, as explained in earlier lessons, whatever is positioned next to the verb is
emphasized. For example:

ང་ལ་འཁྱུ་ཡག་གི་དུག་སློག་མང་པོ་ཡོད།
I have lots of clothes to be washed (not plates or anything else).
