Finally, all the nominalizers are also used to build relative clauses — the subject of the next lesson.

The term བཞེན་པ།
There are varying opinions on whether བཞེན་པ་ is

anominalizer or not. It is added to the verb in the

Present tense in order to express an ongoing action. It is most commonly translated into English
using the “‘-ing” form of the verb (16. the Present Continuous). For example:

ང་སློབ་སྦྱོང་བྱེད་བཞིན་པ་ཡིན།

I'm studying (these days, or right now)

ཁོང་ཁ་ལག་བཟོ་བཞིན་པ་རེད།

(S)he is cooking.

Note: only ཡིན། and རེད། are used after བཞིན་པ་་

To sum
it up
The explanations given in this lesson are an introduction to the ways in which nominalizers are
commonly used within the Tibetan exile community.
However, it is important to be aware that certain dialects entail completely different usages. To
introduce them all here would go beyond the scope of this (introductory) text book.
