Nominalizers
The Tibetan language makes use of a variety of nominalizers. These syllables are added to a verb to
turn it into a noun. The four most frequently used are པ་, ཡག, མཁན་ and ས་. The less common
nominalizers, such as སྟངས་, ཚུལ་, སོལ་, will be introduced in Lesson 23.
a) Functions of nominalizer ཡག or རྒྱུ།
The nominalizer ཡག is sometimes also pronounced as “ye” and has various functions. It cannot
therefore be translated by any single word. Be aware that རྒྱུ་ can also be used in place of ཡག in
some circumstances (see examples below). The verb preceding ཡག is always in the present tense.
Infinitive
The first among the different functions of ཡག is to construct the infinitive. Note: in literary Tibetan,
the syllable པ་ has this same function. For example:
ཉམས་ལེན་བྱེད་ཡག་དེ་གལ་ཆེན་པོ་རེད། (literary: ཉམས་ལེན་བྱེད་པ་དེ་གལ་ཆེ།) It is important to
practice. or: Practicing is important.
ང་གཞས་གཏོང་ཡག་ལ་དགའ་པོ་ཞེ་དྲགས་ཡོད།
གཞས་གཏོང་རྒྱུ་ལ་དགའ་པོ་ཞེ་དྲགས་ཡོད།
I really like singing.
ལས་ཀ་བྱེད་ཡག་དེ་ལས་འཁམ་འཁམ་ལ་འགོ་ཡག་དེ་སིད་པ་ཡོད་ས་རེད།
It must be more pleasant to go for a walk than to work.
The above examples are straightforward. However, it is important to remember that when there is a
verb of motion གར་ replaces ཡག. See Lesson 9. For example:
དེ་རིང་ང་ང་རང་གི་རོ་རོ་ལགས་མཇལ་གར་འགོ་གི་ཡིན།
Today I will go to see /go and see (my) grandmother.
Future object
The second (nominalizer) function of ཡག is to direct the nominalized verb towards the future. For
example:
དེ་རིང་ང་ལ་དེབ་སན་པོ་ཅིག་ཀོག་ཡག་ཡོད།
Today I have an interesting book to read.
