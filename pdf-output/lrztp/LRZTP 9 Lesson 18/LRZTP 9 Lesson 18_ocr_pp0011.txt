c) Nominalizer མཁན།
The nominalizer མཁན་ indicates the agent of the verb. The verb which precedes it is in the Present
tense. It can indicate both the Future (as introduced in Lesson 7) and the Present tense. In this
context, the boundaries between these two tenses are quite fluid. For example:
ང་ཡང་གསུང་ཆོས་ལ་འགྲོ་མཁན་ཡིན།

I am also going to the Dharma teaching.

འདིར་བཞུགས་མཁན་ཡོད་པས། སྒོ་དེ་ཕྱི་གནང་རོགས་གནང་།
Is anyone here? Please open the door!

d) Nominalizer ས།
When the term a, which literally means

"earth", "place" or "ground," is added to a verb, it shows

location. It indicates where the action of the verb is taking place, where it originates from, where it

is directed towards. As with BYR, the verb that precedes the a is in the Present tense. Note: the
translation doesn't always take account of these terms. For example:

བློ་བཟང་ཇ་འཐུང་ས་ལ་ཕྱིན་སོང་།
Lobsang went to the tea shop.

མགྲོན་པོ་མང་པོ་སླེབས་ཙང་ཞལ་ས་ཤུང་དྲགས་སོང་།
Since many guests arrived, there was not enough space in which to sleep.
