e) In English, one says things like “Don't forget!” In Tibetan, there is a particular
form that is used when the verb is བྱེད་མེད་ལས་ཚིག་ (non-volitional). The phrase
པར་བྱེད། is added to the verb or its negation, and it can be translated as “Make sure ...
does (not) happen!” Depending on the (suffix of the) verb, it can also be བར་བྱེད།
(After no suffix, suffixes, ལ་, ང་ and ར་) Often it is followed by the imperative
marker ཨ།, which could be translated into an exclamation mark.
Formula: (Negation) + Verb (Present Tense) + པར་བྱེད་ + ཨ།
For example:
ཚིག་གསར་པ་དེ་ཚོ་ཧ་གོ་བར་བྱེད་ཨ། Make sure you know the new words!
ཁོང་གི་མཚན་དེ་ཤེས་པར་བྱེད་ཨ།
Make sure you know his/her name! (H)
སོབ་ཚན་འདི་དན་པར་བྱེད་ཨ།
Make sure you remember this lesson!
ཅ་ལག་འདི་མ་བརླག་པར་བྱེད་ཨ།
Make sure you do not lose this!
ཚིག་དེ་ཚོ་མ་བརེད་པར་བྱེད་ཨ།
(Make sure you) do not forget these words!
ཨ་མས་མ་མཐོང་བར་བྱེད་ཨ།
(Make sure you) do not let mother see it!
The Imperative Combinations
Note that the above forms and imperative markers are often combined with each
other and, thereby, cover the whole range from very polite requests to orders
delivered to a child. Exact English translations may be lacking, particularly in the
context of the honorific. For example:
ཁེད་རང་ཡིན་གཅིག་མིན་གཅིག་ཐུགས་སོ་ལ་ཕེབས་ཤོག I'm urging you, please come
to the party! (H)
ཕག་དེབ་འདི་གཅིག་གཟིགས་དང་།
Please have a look at this book! (H)
