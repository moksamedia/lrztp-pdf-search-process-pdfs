a) Auxiliaries
Tibetan language makes use of auxiliaries to express the different times and modes of the verbs (such
as volitional, direct experience, etc.). The auxiliaries are added to the main verb, some verbs have a

special form for the past, such as: འགྲོ་ (present) - ཕྱིན་ (past), ཟྲ་ (present) - བཟས། (past) - others are the
same in the three times: ཆཐོང་་, ཀློག་: etc. For example:
ཁོང་སློབ་གྲྭ་ལ་འགྲོ་གི་འདུག

འགྲོ་ = main verb (present), གི་ = འབྲེལ་སྒྲ་: འདུག verb to be/have

(S)he goes to school./(S)he is going to school.
The auxiliaries of present and future consist of the verb "to have" and "to be" added to a འབྲེལ་སྒྲ་
(connective particle) and have the same notions! ARR = གི་འདུག = direct knowledge (གཞན་).
