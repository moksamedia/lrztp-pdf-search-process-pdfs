ལོ་ཙཱ་བ་རིན་ཆེན་བཟང་པོའ་ི སྐད་སྒྱུར་སོབ་གྲྭའི་སྐབས་དགུ་པ།

You already know...
1) How to read and pronounce any Tibetan word! (For your exam no need to know the theoretical
details about the script, such as parts of the syllable...).
The few exceptions are:
a) ལྷ་ Usually the head letters are NOT PRONOUNCED, but here the ལ་མགོ་ (head letter ལ་) is
combined with the main letter ཧ་:
ལྷ་ས་ (Lhasa, place of the God(s))
ལྷ་ཁང་ temple
གཟའ་ལྷག་པ་ Wednesday
b) ཟླ་བ་ TOTALLY random! Usually the ལ་བཏགས་ (subscribed ལ་) is just pronounced as ལ་ (for
example: གོག་ཀླད་) But for s o m e reason, main letter ཟ་ with subscribed ལ་ turns into a da
sound ཟླ་བ་ = Dawa, (name of a person) month, moon.
c) བ་ is a bit funny... When combined with a prefix ད་ OR when being the 2nd or 3rd part of a
word (particle), it is pronounced as ཝ་. For example:
དབང་གྲགས་ Wangdak ཨ་རི་བ་ (American) རྒྱ་གར་བ་ (Indian) ཟླ་བ་ (Dawa)
However, when the བ་ is the main letter or suffix in a syllable, it is pronounced properly
བུ་མོ་ = girl, བ་ཕྱུགས་ cow
Furthermore: When you have a combination of the prefix ད་, main letter བ་ (+ vowel) and the
subscribed ཡ་, you pronounce only the vowel and the subscribed letter! དབིན་ཇི་ = yinji
(foreigner, lit. British) or འཇམ་དབངས་ལགས་ (Jamyang-la) (sweet sound, melody) also name of
the Buddha of Wisdom: འཇམ་དཔལ་དབངས་ (Manjushri).
d) Exception: When the vowel is with the particle འི་ (འབེལ་སྒྲ་ connective particle), it changes the
vowels into an Umlaut - just like suffixes ས་ and ད་.
For example: ངའི་ (my, mine) ང་ཚོའི་ (our, ours) མོའི་ (her, hers).
