The main postpositions
In English, as their name suggests, prepositions precede the noun or pronoun that they
qualify. “Pre” means "before" in Latin. For example: on the table. In Tibetan, however, these
words follow the noun or pronoun that they qualify and they are therefore called
postpositions. “Post” means "after" in Latin.
Technically speaking, there should always be a འབྟེལ་ས་ (connective particle) between the
noun/pronoun and its postposition, although in spoken Tibetan this is sometimes dropped.
The particle ལ་ is used to indicate the location or “goal” of the action. The most common
postpositions are:
ནང་ in, inside
ཕི་ལློགས་ outside
འློག
under, underneath
སང་
on top of
བར་ between
སློར་
about, around
རྒྱབ་ behind
མདུན་
in front of
འཁིས་ next, near, close to
གཡས་གཡློན་ around (lit. right and left)
ཆྟེད་དུ་ for, in order to, for the sake of
Formula for postpositions:
noun / pronoun
དགློན་པ་

+ འབྟེལ་ས་ + postposition
+ འི་
+ ནང་

+ ལ་
+ ལ་

For example:
ལྷ་ཁང་གི་ནང་ལ་སྐུ་འདྲ་དང་དཔྟེ་ཆ་མང་པློ་ཡློད་རྟེད།
There are many statues and pechas inside the temple.
ལྡྟེ་མིག་ག་པར་འདུག ལྡྟེ་མིག་ཅློག་ཙེའ་ི སང་ལ་བཞག་འདུག
Where are the keys? They are (lit. have been put) on the table.
ངའི་སློབ་གྲྭའི་མདུན་ལ་སྤང་སིང་རྟེ་པློ་ཅིག་ཡློད་རྟེད།
There is a lovely meadow in front of my school.
པུ་གུ་ཚོ་ཤིང་སྡློང་གི་རྒྱབ་ལ་ཡིབ་གར་འགློ་གི་འདུག
The children go to hide behind the tree.
