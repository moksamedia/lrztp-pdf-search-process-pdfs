It is a peculiarity of the Tibetan language that there are different registers depending on the level of
politeness required. Among the personal pronouns, ཁེད་རྣམ་(པ་)ཚོ་ and ཁོང་རྣམ་(པ་)ཚོ་ are the most
respectful. ཁེད་རང་ and ཁོང་ are also very polite, whilst རང་, ཁོ་, མོ་ and ཁོ་རང་ཚོ་ are more familiar
and used with people one is close to. Be aware that the pronouns are perceived differently in the
different regions of Tibet. For example, ཁོད་,མོ་, ཁོ་ etc. are common pronouns in Eastern Tibet but
can be considered impolite in Lhasa. This illustrates that there is probably no general rule on how
to use pronouns, but you should be aware of your choices and their implications.
Also: ཁོད་ in literary Tibetan is polite, not informal, which is good to keep in mind when reciting:
ཁོད་ནི་བླ་མ། ཁོད་ནི་སངས་རྒྱས། = You are the lama, you are the Buddha.
However: In colloquial, it is not polite, it shows closeness.
