LRZTP 9 Module 3 – Lesson 23, November 2023

Grammatical aims of this lesson
After studying this lesson you will know:
• that there are alternative ways of expressing the sequence of two actions (using བྱས།, ནས།
and སྟེ།)
• that sometimes བྱས།, ནས། and སྟེ་ can also show a reason
• how to use བྱས།,ནས། and སྟེ། to form adverbs
• how to combine them with verbs of motion
• the additional functions of ནས། (from, since, among, etc.)
• how to use བྱས། when making a list
• that དེ།, སྟེ།, ཏེ།, when added to a complete sentence, also mean “but”
Sequence and reason
It is convenient to introduce the connectives བྱས།, ནས། and སྟེ། at the same time, as many of their
functions overlap. In this way, they can be compared and contrasted with each other. Please be
aware that people from Lhasa tend to favour བྱས་. The more literary forms are ཏེ།, སྟེ། and དེ།. The
most universal form is ནས།. In most of their usages, these connectives follow after a verb in the past
tense. In this context, the second part of the sentence can be in any tense or mode. The first usage
carries the meaning of “after” or “because.” For example:
ངའི་ཨ་མས་ང་ལ་བཀའ་བཀོན་གནང་ནས་ང་སེམས་པ་སྐྱོ་བྱུང་། བཀའ་བཀོན་གནང་བྱས་ བཀའ་བཀོན་གནང་ཏེ་
I felt sad because (or "after") my mother scolded me.
In such a context, བྱས། or སྟེ། would carry exactly the same meaning. For example:
ཁོང་ཆང་མང་པོ་བཏུང་ནས་ར་བཟི་སོང་།
After drinking lots of chang, he got drunk.
བསྟན་འཛིན་ལགས་ཀིས་དཀའ་ལས་མང་པོ་བརྒྱབ་བྱས་འབྲས་བུ་ཡག་པོ་སིན་པ་རེད།
Afterwards, because Tenzin made great effort, there was a good result.
ན་ནིང་ངའི་ཅོ་ཅོ་ཆང་ས་བརྒྱབ་བྱས་ཁང་པ་ཡང་སྤོས་སོང་།
