Adverbial Compound - two actions at the same time
Furthermore, the connectives བྱས།, ནས། 3114 སྟེ། can all be used to express the fact that two actions are
happening at the same time, in which case they form an adverbial compound. For example:

ཁོང་གཟབ་གཟང་བྱས་ནས་འགྲོ་གི་འདུག

ཁོང་གཟབ་གཟང་བྱས་སྟེ་འགྲོ་གི་འདུག

There are two verbs in this sentence: "to be careful" and "to go." Rather than meaning "after being
careful, he went," this sentence is to be understood as "he went carefully." The verb that is followed

by བྱས, ནས་ OF སྟེ་ elaborates on the manner in which the other verb is happening. For example:

བཀྲ་ཤིས་དེབ་ཀློག་བྱས་ནང་ལ་བསྡད་འདུག
Tashi is staying at home reading books.
