དུས་འདས་པ། THE PAST TENSE
Simple Past
The Tibetan language makes use of two types of past tense, namely: the simple past (I went)
and the present perfect (I have been / have gone). There are no particular auxiliaries for the
past perfect (I had gone). Since the present perfect tense, as its name tells, can also be
perceived as a present tense, it will be introduced in the coming (present tense) lesson.
Each of these two tenses is expressed by means of a different set of auxiliaries as you saw
in the table in Lesson 5.
