The connector ཙང་ : “because”, “since”, “as”, “therefore”, etc.
Position and use
In Tibetan, the connector (or conjunction) "because" cannot be placed at the beginning of a
sentence. The construction is similar to the way a conditional phrase is formed, using ན་.
To indicate the speaker's perspective, only those auxiliaries pertaining to བདག་, such as
ཡིན་, མིན་, ཡོད་, etc. can precede the connector ཙང་. You will never hear somebody saying:
*རེད་ཙང་ because རེད། is considered an objective, set, closed statement which therefore does
not serve for being a reason.
The formula:
reason + ཙང་ + result
Alternative versions
So, according to the formula, the sentence is constructed like this:
བློ་བཟང་བོད་པ་ཡིན་ + ཙང་ + བོད་ཇ་མང་པོ་འཐུང་གི་ཡོད་རེད།
Because Lobsang is Tibetan (reason), he drinks lots of Tibetan tea (result).
This causal relationship could equally be expressed in two sentences, in which case the
second one would start with བས་ཙང་ (therefore):
བློ་བཟང་བོད་པ་རེད། བས་ཙང་ཁོང་བོད་ཇ་མང་པོ་འཐུང་གི་ཡོད་རེད།
Lobsang is (a) Tibetan. Therefore he drinks a lot of Tibetan tea.
Or one could first state the result and then add གང་ཡིན་ཟེར་ན་ (“because”), which literally
means “if one asks why”:
བློ་བཟང་བོད་ཇ་མང་པོ་འཐུང་གི་ཡོད་རེད། གང་ཡིན་ཟེར་ན་ཁོང་བོད་པ་རེད།
Lobsang drinks lots of Tibetan tea because he is (a) Tibetan.
To be even more elaborate, one could also use the expression: རྒྱུ་མཚན་ག་རེ་རེད་ཟེར་ན་– “if one
asks the reason.”
བློ་བཟང་བོད་ཇ་མང་པོ་འཐུང་གི་ཡོད་རེད། རྒྱུ་མཚན་ག་རེ་རེད་ཟེར་ན་ཁོང་བོད་པ་རེད།
Lobsang drinks a lot of Tibetan tea. Why? He is Tibetan.
