LRZTP 9 Module 1 – Lesson 7, June 2023
Practically Speaking
So, all you need to know: Replace the ཀི་ by a བསད་ and talk about ongoing actions of
both oneself and others. It is probably safe to say that using བསད་ makes the ongoing
action even more explicit.
...And remember: The ཞེ་ས་ of བསད་པ་ is བཞུགས་པ་…
Some More Examples
 ང་ཚོ་ཚང་མ་ནང་ལ་བསད་བསད་ཡོད། = We are all staying at home.
 ཁེད་རང་ད་ལྟ་ག་རེ་གནང་བསད་ཡོད། = What are you doing now?
 ཁོང་ཚོས་དེང་སང་མཆོད་རྟེན་གསར་པ་ཅིག་བཞེངས་བསད་འདུག = These days they are
building a new stupa.
 ཕི་ལོགས་ལ་ཆར་པ་ཤུགས་ཆེན་པོ་ཅིག་བཏང་བསད་འདུག = Outside it is raining hard.
 ད་ལྟ་བོ་བཟང་པར་ཅིག་བརྒྱབ་བསད་འདུག = Now Lobsang is taking a picture.
 ངའི་འཛིན་གྲྭ་གཅིག་པ་དེ་ཚོ་སྐད་ཆ་བཤད་བསད་འདུག = My classmates are chatting.
 རྒན་ལགས་ཕག་དེབ་ཅིག་གཟིགས་བཞུགས་འདུག = The teacher is reading a book (H).
It cannot be said to be a fixed rule, but in terms of English translation, for an action
happening right now, present continuous is often used.
ཁོང་ད་ལྟ་ནང་ལས་བྱེད་ཀི་འདུག
(S)he is doing housework now.
As opposed to:
ཁོང་རྟག་པར་ནང་ལས་མང་པོ་བྱེད་ཀི་ཡོད་རེད།
(S)he always does a lot of housework.
As a very general rule, it is safe to say that probably more often than not, present tense is
used for habitual actions rather than something happening right now.
ཁེད་རང་ཤ་ཟ་གི་ཡོད་པས།
This sentence can mean two different things:
Are you eating meat? Or: Do you eat meat?
