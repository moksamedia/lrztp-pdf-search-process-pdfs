Additional Grammar Note:
It is another peculiarity of the present tense that it may be used in the past if the situation
requires it. What would be such a case? If we talk about something we used to do
habitually in the past, we would use the present tense auxiliaries:
ང་ལོ་ཆུང་ཆུང་ཡིན་དུས་རོགས་པ་མཉམ་དུ་རེད་མོ་མང་པོ་རེ་གི་ཡོད།
When I was small, I used to play with my friends a lot.
Usage of the Agentive Marker བྱེད་སྒྲ། (Review)
As introduced in the previous lesson, the བྱེད་སྒྲ་ is generally not required in the present tense.
Remember, however, that there are two exceptions to this. There are two occasions where it
should be used in the present tense:
1) When there is potential confusion about who the agent and who the object is. For
example:
ངས་ཁེད་རང་མཐོང་གི་མི་འདུག
I don’t see you.
The reason for the potential doer-object confusion is that the auxiliary གི་འདུག can be used for
any person in the case of a non-volitional verb.
2) Where something is being emphasized. For example:
དེང་སང་ངས་སོབ་སོང་བྱེད་ཀི་ཡོད།
These days I study. Or: These days, I am studying.
ངའི་ཅོ་ཅོས་ལོད་ལོད་བྱས་ནས་བསད་ཀི་འདུག
My brother is relaxing: My brother relaxes.
དེང་སང་ངའི་དགའ་རོགས་ཀིས་ཁ་ལག་བཟོ་གི་འདུག
These days, my boyfriend does the cooking.
ངས་ཁང་པ་གཙང་མ་བཟོ་གི་ཡོད།
I do the house cleaning.
