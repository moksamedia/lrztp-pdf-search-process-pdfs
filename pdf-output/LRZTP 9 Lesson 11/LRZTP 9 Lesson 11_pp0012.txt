བྱེད་མེད་ལས་ཚིག Non-volitional
These five most common secondary verbs are considered non-volitional and are treated and
conjugated accordingly. You can think of them as being a result, for example: ཐུབ་པ་ and ཤེས་
པ་ or a feeling in the case of འདོད་པ་. To think of them as བྱེད་མེད་ལས་ཚིག་ will help you to
choose the correct auxiliaries.
Auxiliaries
The auxiliaries are combined with secondary verbs in the same way as they are combined
with full verbs. For example, ཡོད། expresses a habitual mode, འདུག an immediate, direct
experience, གི་རེད། a future action, etc.
a. The secondary verb ཐུབ་པ།
The secondary verb ཐུབ་པ་ is translated as "can", "to be able to", "to have the ability to", "to
manage", etc. It talks about the physical, technical or mental ability of someone.
ཐུབ་པ། in the Future:
མ་འོངས་པར་ངས་བོད་ཡིག་ཀོག་ཐུབ་ཀི་རེད།
In the future, I will be able to read literary Tibetan.
ལོ་རེས་མར་ཁེད་རང་བ་མའི་གསུང་ཆོས་ཧ་གོ་ཐུབ་ཀི་རེད།
Next year, you will be able to understand the lama’s teachings.
སང་ཉིན་གི་བར་དུ་ང་ལས་ཀ་འདི་ཚར་ཐུབ་ཀི་མ་རེད།
I won't manage to finish this work until tomorrow.
ཐུབ་པ། in the Present:
ང་ཅོག་ཙེ་འདི་ཡར་བཀག་ཐུབ་ཀི་མི་འདུག ལིད་ཁོག་ཚ་པོ་ཞེ་དྲགས་འདུག
I cannot lift this table. It is very heavy.
(I have noticed this right now, as I’m trying to lift it.)
ང་ཉིན་མ་རག་པར་ཕག་འཚལ་ཐུབ་ཀི་མེད། རྐང་པ་ན་གི་རེད།
I am unable to do prostrations every day. I will have aching legs.
(General, habitual, with no action taking place right now)
པུ་གུ་ཆུང་ཆུང་དེ་ཚོ་ཤིང་སྡོང་སྒང་ལ་འཛེག་ཐུབ་ཀི་ཡོད་མ་རེད།
Small children do not have the ability to climb trees.
(Inferential, general statement, with no action taking place)
