Introduction
The Tibetan language makes use of several secondary verbs. Not all of these are used with
equal frequency, so this lesson will only introduce the most common five, namely ga", ཤེས་
Ay", འདོད་པ་: དགོས་པ་ and རྱོང་བ་ —

“to be able to”, “to know how to do”, “to want’,

“to have

to” and “to have the experience of”.
Definition
A secondary verb, as the name implies, always follows after the main or full verb. However,
the secondary verb དགོས་པ་ can also function as a full verb. For example:

ང་ལ་སྒུ་གུ་དགོས།

I need/want a pen.

ང་སྒྱ་གུ་ཉོ་དགོས་ཡོད།

I need to buy a pen.
