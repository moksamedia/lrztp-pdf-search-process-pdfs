c. The secondary verb འདོད་པ།
The verb འདོད་པ་ is translated as "to want to", "to wish to", "would like to", "to have the wish
to", etc. There is also a stronger form, སིང་འདོད་, which means "to really want to", "to love
to", "to crave", etc.
There are no particular future forms for expressing a wish, because the thought itself is
happening in the present. Therefore, one uses present tense auxiliaries when talking about the
future. Note: in the Present tense, the གི་ element of the auxiliary is mostly omitted. For example:
ང་བོད་ལ་འགོ་འདོད་ཡོད།
(not: བོད་ལ་འགོ་འདོད་ཀི་ཡོད།)
I want to go to Tibet.
You could also think of this as the verb “to have the wish to” go to Tibet.
ང་ལ་བོད་ལ་འགོ་(ཡག་གི་)འདོད་(པ་)ཡོད།
In this way, one commonly says:
འདོད་ཡོད།, འདོད་མེད།, འདོད་ཡོད་རེད།, འདོད་ཡོད་མ་རེད།, etc.
– omitting the གི་ that would otherwise follow འདོད་.
འདོད་པ་ in the Present
མོག་མོག་འདི་ཚོ་ཞིམ་པོ་མི་འདུག བྱས་ཙང་ང་ཟ་སིང་འདོད་ཀི་མི་འདུག
These momos are not tasty, so I don't feel like eating them.
Note: This could also be expressed as:
མོག་མོག་འདི་ཚོ་ཞིམ་པོ་མི་འདུག བྱས་ཙང་ང་ཟ་འདོད་མེད།/མི་འདུག
The first version is more pointedly referring to the present moment, whereas the second
version is more general.
