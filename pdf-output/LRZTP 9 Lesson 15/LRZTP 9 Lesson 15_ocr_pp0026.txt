These auxiliaries do not involve direct evidence, either. It is tricky to talk about degrees of
certainty and probability.

But because

there is no evidence, people

consider that these

auxiliaries express /ess likelihood than ས་རེད། and པ་འདྲ།. They can even express a mere
guess. They are therefore often translated as "maybe,

perhaps," etc.

Examples with "to be" and "to have":

བློ་བཟང་ཁ་ཆེ་ཡིན་གྱི་རེད།
Perhaps Lobsang is a Muslim.

There are probably lots of poor people in India.

Perhaps he does not have a wife.
